### **Q: Why is the plasma membrane called a selectively permeable membrane?**

**Answer:**  
The plasma membrane is called a selectively permeable membrane because:
1. It allows only **specific substances** to pass in and out of the cell.
2. **Essential nutrients** (like glucose, amino acids, oxygen) can enter the cell.
3. **Waste products** (like carbon dioxide, urea) can exit the cell.
4. It **blocks harmful or unnecessary substances** from entering.
5. This selective control helps maintain a **stable internal environment (homeostasis)** necessary for cell survival.
Hence, the plasma membrane acts like a guard of the cell, so it is called **selectively permeable**.

### **Q. Which organelle is known as the powerhouse of the cell? Why?**
**Answer:**
- The **mitochondria** are called the **powerhouse of the cell**.
- They are double-membrane bound organelles present in most eukaryotic cells.
- Mitochondria carry out **cellular respiration**, in which food (glucose) is broken down in the presence of oxygen to release energy.
- This energy is stored in the form of **ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)** molecules, also known as the **energy currency of the cell**.
- Cells use ATP to perform all life activities like growth, movement, transport, and reproduction.
Since mitochondria produce and supply energy required for all cellular activities, they are called the **powerhouse of the cell**.

### **Q. What is Osmosis?**
**Answer:**
- **Osmosis** is the movement of **water molecules** from a region of **higher water concentration (dilute solution)** to a region of **lower water concentration (concentrated solution)** through a **selectively permeable membrane**.
- It is a **special type of diffusion** that only involves water.
- Example:
    - Absorption of water by root hairs from soil.
    - Movement of water into raisins when placed in water.
- Osmosis is essential for processes like **transport of water in plants**, **maintaining cell turgidity**, and **exchange of fluids in animal cells**.
Osmosis helps maintain the balance of water in cells and is vital for the survival of living organisms.

### **Q. What is a tissue?**
- A **tissue** is a group of cells that are **similar in structure** and work together to perform a **specific function**.
- Example: Muscle tissue helps in movement, and xylem tissue helps in water transport in plants.

### **Q. What is the utility of tissues in multicellular organisms?**
**Answer:**  
Tissues are useful in multicellular organisms because:
1. They allow **division of labour** ‚Äì different tissues perform different functions (e.g., nerve tissue for impulses, muscle tissue for movement).
2. Increase in **efficiency** ‚Äì work is carried out more effectively when cells specialize.
3. Help in the **organization of body structure**.
4. Enable multicellular organisms to perform **complex functions**.
5. Provide **support, protection, and coordination** in the body.
Tissues help multicellular organisms survive by ensuring that different life processes are carried out efficiently.

### **Q. Define the term ‚Äútissue‚Äù.**
A **tissue** is a group of cells that are similar in structure and work together to perform a specific function.  
_Example:_ Muscle tissue helps in movement, and xylem tissue helps in conduction of water in plants.



### **Q. How are simple tissues different from complex tissues in plants?**

|**Simple Tissues**|**Complex Tissues**|
|---|---|
|Made up of **only one type of cell**.|Made up of **more than one type of cell**.|
|All cells perform the **same function**.|Different cells perform **different functions** together.|
|Examples: Parenchyma, Collenchyma, Sclerenchyma.|Examples: Xylem, Phloem.|

### **Q. What is the specific function of the cardiac muscle?**
- Cardiac muscle is **involuntary** and found only in the **walls of the heart**.
- Its specific function is to **rhythmically contract and relax throughout life**, thereby pumping blood to all parts of the body.
- It works **continuously without fatigue**.
